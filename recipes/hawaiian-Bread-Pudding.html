Hawaiian Bread Pudding

Description

Hawaiian Bread Pudding is the perfect use for leftover bread! This soft custard, loaded with tropical flavors and a fruity caramel sauce, makes a great dessert or snack.

Ingredients

  • 1 package (16 ounces) pan de leche or pandesal rolls
  • 1 can (8 ounces) pineapple tidbits, drained and juice reserved
  • ¼ cup raisins
  • 1 ½ cups Nestle's all-purpose cream
  • 1 can (14 ounces) evaporated milk
  • 6 eggs
  • 2 cups sugar
  • 1 tablespoon melted butter
  • 2 tablespoons unsweetened shredded coconut

Caramel Sauce

  • 4 tbsp butter
  • ¼ cup brown sugar
  • ¼ cup pineapple juice (reserved from can of pineapples)
  • ½ cup Nestle's all-purpose cream
  • ⅓ tsp nutmeg
  • ⅓ tsp cinnamon

Steps

  1. In a large bowl, combine bread cubes, pineapple chunks, and raisins. Transfer to a lightly greased 9" x 11" baking dish.
  2. In a bowl, combine all-purpose cream, evaporated milk, eggs, sugar, and melted butter. Whisk until well-blended.
  3. Pour liquid mixture over bread mixture. Gently toss to moisten bread.
  4. Cover the baking dish with foil and refrigerate for one to two hours.
  5. Remove cover and sprinkle coconut flakes on top of bread mixture.
  6. Bake in a 350 F oven for about 50 to 60 minutes or until a toothpick inserted in the middle comes out clean.
  7. Remove from heat and allow to slightly cool.
  8. Drizzle caramel syrup over pudding and cut into squares. Serve warm or cold.

For the Caramel Sauce

  1. In a saucepan over medium heat, melt butter.
  2. Add sugar and stir until dissolved.
  3. Add pineapple juice and all-purpose cream, stirring constantly.
  4. Add cinnamon and nutmeg and stir to distribute.
  5. Continue to cook for about 1 to 2 minutes or until sauce is slightly thickened.
